Decision-making isn't just a business buzzword; it's a complex process we navigate every day. I created Win-Sets to dive into this complexity through the lens of political science theory. This repo is less about code and more about exploring how we make choices in various contexts—specifically within institutions and individuals. 

## What Is Win-Sets?

At its core, Win-Sets is about understanding the mechanics behind decision-making. It brings together theoretical concepts from political science and personal reflections to shed light on how external pressures and societal norms influence our choices. Think of it as a conceptual playground for anyone curious about the dynamics of decision-making.

### Why This Project Exists

I built Win-Sets because decision-making isn't always rational or straightforward. In institutions, decisions often hinge on players' perceived interests. By grounding my exploration in established theories, I can analyze the nuances of how choices are made—especially under stress. Whether it’s a last-minute deadline at work or navigating social pressures, my aim is to examine these everyday scenarios through a theoretical lens.

## Key Design Decisions

This project isn't a typical software repository; it's primarily a conceptual exploration. I chose to document my findings and reflections using Markdown. Here are a few intentional design choices I made:

- **Focus on Clarity**: I wanted the concepts to be accessible, so I kept the structure simple. The main document (`index.md`) serves as the hub for everything.
- **Personal Insights**: Alongside theoretical explorations, I included personal anecdotes. These reflections add a layer of real-world relevance and help ground the theory in lived experiences.
- **No Code**: Unlike most repos, there isn’t a codebase here. This decision stems from the project's purpose—it's about understanding concepts rather than executing them programmatically.

## Tech Stack

- **Markdown**: Simple and effective for documenting complex ideas without unnecessary fluff. I love its versatility for both writing and sharing.
- **No Programming Languages**: It's all about theory and insights, not software development—though I’m open to ideas if I decide to pivot later.
